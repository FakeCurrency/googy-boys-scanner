How the scanner works

Every trading day the scanner reviews the ASX and NASDAQ and surfaces stocks showing a healthy uptrend that has paused and pulled back to a key moving average. It is a shortlist for manual review — it does not place trades or predict prices.

The Fibonacci-EMA framework

The engine tracks seven exponential moving averages spaced along the Fibonacci sequence:

8›13›21› 34›55›89›144

The ideal condition is when they line up in order — the fast 8 above the 13, above the 21, and so on. That is full bullish alignment: a clean uptrend across every timescale at once.

Signal chips

Each pattern element a stock shows contributes points toward a score out of 13:

  • FULL BULLISH ALIGNMENT all seven EMAs stacked in order. +3
  • CORE FIB PULLBACK EMA_34 price has retraced to a core EMA (21 / 34 / 55). +3
  • STRONG FIB CONFLUENCE @ … several EMA levels cluster at one price zone. +3
  • EMA COMPRESSION the moving averages are bunched tightly. +2
  • WEEKLY BULLISH the weekly timeframe is also trending up. +1
  • VOLUME EXPANSION volume is elevated versus its recent average. +1

Grades

The grade is simply the sum of the points above (out of 13).

A+ The most signals stacked together — the strongest aligned setup. Tradeable
A A strong setup with most quality signals present. Tradeable
B A partial setup — fewer signals lined up. Watching
C Only one or two signals present. Watching

Entry, stop & target

  • Entry — the pullback level, where price has retraced to a key EMA.
  • Stop — below the recent swing low / structure (shown as Stop %).
  • Target — the nearest real resistance above; if price is at new highs the target falls back to a 2R measured move, tagged TARGET = 2R FALLBACK.
  • P2 — the Phase-2 SuperTrend (ATR) trailing stop, as a % below price.

The R : R is reward-to-risk. A tradeable A+ / A grade must also clear 1.5 : 1 — strong patterns with a weaker reward-to-risk are demoted to the Watch list and flagged LOW R:R, because backtesting showed a minimum R:R materially improves the strategy.

PULSE & liquidity

The PULSE bar across the top tracks macro context — gold, silver, oil, gas, the tech and biotech indices, bond yields and the Aussie dollar — each with its 1-day and 5-day move. A liquidity filter keeps only names trading meaningful volume each day; the most liquid are tagged LIQUID.

The Reversals scanner

The Reversals tab is a second, separate scanner for the *opposite* setup — catching the birth of a new uptrend rather than a pullback within an existing one. It looks for a beaten-down or basing stock turning up, using SMA 9/26/43/200, RSI 14 and Volume. Each setup scores out of 14:

  • 9 › 26 RECLAIM price crossing up through the short SMAs (the trigger). +4
  • BASE / DOWNTREND well off its one-year high — room to run. +3
  • VOLUME SURGE volume expanding vs the 20-day average. +3
  • BASE BREAKOUT closing above the base high / descending resistance. +2
  • RSI TURNING UP RSI rising through its signal line. +2

Same grades (A+ ≥ 11, A ≥ 9, B ≥ 6, C ≥ 4), levels, dropdown and chart — switch with the Pullbacks / Reversals toggle in the header.

Timing & data

Scans run automatically after each market's close — ASX around 6:00 am AEST, NASDAQ after the US session. Three markets are covered: ASX, NASDAQ and Crypto (the top 100 coins by market cap, which trade 24/7). Price and volume data comes from Yahoo Finance; the crypto list comes from CoinGecko.
