How the scanner works

VIVEK 5.0 looks for one repeatable event: price reacting at its 200-period SMA — the line that separates trend from no-trend. It scans the ASX, NASDAQ and Crypto, and hands you a shortlist with a ready-made trade plan to review. It doesn't predict prices or place trades.

The setup

Price trends away from the 200 SMA, then returns to test it. The level either holds (a long, trend resumes) or fails (a short, trend flips). It's read on two timeframes — Weekly (primary; slower, highest-conviction) and Daily / H4 (timing) — and only becomes ARMED once a trigger actually fires. Until then it's WATCHING: near the level, nothing yet.

The trigger — three entry types

Every armed setup is one of three reactions, labelled on the card and on every trade:

  • RECLAIM pierced the SMA, was rejected, then closed back on the trend side.
  • RETEST returned to the level and confirmed it with momentum.
  • BREAK broke minor structure sitting right at the level.

Filter by these under 200 SMA interaction; the Track page shows expectancy per entry type.

Levels change with timeframe

  • Daily & Weekly each carry their own plan — level, entry, stop and targets — so Entry / SL / TP1–TP3 update when you switch timeframe on the chart.
  • 4H & 3D show real candles but borrow the Daily plan's levels (no separate plan yet) — flagged with a notice on the chart.

Grades

A+ Cleanest, highest-conviction reaction. Tradeable
A Strong reaction, most signals present. Tradeable
B+ Partial setup, fewer signals. Watch list
WATCH Near the level / informational. Watching

Quality is scored on alignment, structure, volume, weekly confluence and momentum. An A+/A is demoted to B+ if it isn't armed or its R:R to TP2 is under 1.5 : 1 (flagged LOW R:R).

The trade plan

  • Entry — the trigger price on the selected timeframe.
  • SL — just beyond the reaction, so a true level failure invalidates it.
  • TP1 / TP2 / TP3 — prior structure first, measured R-multiples in clean air; headline R:R is to TP2.
  • Scale & trail — book partials at each TP (e.g. 25 / 50 / 15%); SL to break-even at TP1, then below new support at TP2, never against the trade.

Journal & track record

Armed A+/A setups are logged to a forward-test journal at the live (delayed) intraday price, then marked to market each scan — scaling and stopping by the rules above. Every trade keeps its entry-type label, timeframe and grade, so the Track page reports real expectancy split by each.

Data

Covers ASX, NASDAQ and Crypto (24/7), refreshing through the session. Prices are from Yahoo Finance — dividend-adjusted, ~15 min delayed — which suits the daily and weekly bars this system trades. The PULSE bar up top tracks macro context (metals, energy, indices, yields, AUD) at a glance.
