How the scanner works

VIVEK 5.0 hunts for one specific, repeatable event: a stock or coin reacting at its 200-period SMA — the line big-picture traders watch as the dividing line between trend and no-trend. The scanner reviews the ASX, NASDAQ and Crypto, finds names interacting with that level, and surfaces a shortlist with a ready-made trade plan for manual review. It does not predict prices, and (today) it does not place trades.

The 200 SMA reaction

The 200 SMA is the only level that matters here. When price has trended away from it and then comes back to test it, one of two things happens: the level holds (a long opportunity as the trend resumes) or it fails (a short as the trend flips). The scanner measures that reaction on two timeframes:

  • Weekly — the primary read. Slower and less noisy; a weekly 200-SMA reaction is the highest-conviction version of the setup.
  • Daily / H4 — the faster read for timing the same level, so an entry can be taken without waiting a full week.

A setup only becomes ARMED (actionable) once a mechanical trigger actually fires at the level — until then it sits as WATCHING: near the 200 SMA, but nothing has happened yet.

The three entry types

Every armed setup is one of exactly three reactions, labelled on the card and on every paper trade so you always know why it triggered:

  • RECLAIM Reclaim after rejection — price pierced the 200 SMA, was rejected, then closed back on the trend side. The level held.
  • RETEST Retest + confirmation — price returns to the level and confirms it (a hold with momentum) rather than slicing through.
  • BREAK Break of small structure — a clean break of a minor swing high/low sitting right at the 200 SMA, confirming the move.

You can filter the dashboard by these under 200 SMA interaction, and the Track page breaks expectancy down by entry type so you can see which reaction is paying.

Timeframe-aware levels

The trade plan is computed independently on each timeframe, so the Entry, SL and TP1–TP3 change when you switch timeframe on the chart:

  • Daily and Weekly each carry their own separate plan — their own 200-SMA level, entry trigger, stop and targets.
  • 4H and 3D are reference views: real 4H / 3-day candles, but they borrow the Daily plan's levels (there's no separate plan at those timeframes yet). The chart shows a clear notice when you're on one of them.

Grades

Each setup is scored on the quality of the reaction (alignment, structure, volume, weekly confluence, momentum) and graded:

A+ Top-tier reaction — the cleanest, highest-conviction setup. Immediately tradeable
A A strong reaction with most quality signals present. Tradeable
B+ A partial setup — fewer signals lined up. Watch list
WATCH Near the level / informational only. Watching

An A+ or A is demoted to B+ if it isn't armed, or if its reward-to-risk to TP2 is below 1.5 : 1 — flagged LOW R:R.

Entry, stop & targets

  • Entry — the trigger price of the reaction (reclaim / retest / break) on the selected timeframe.
  • SL — just beyond the reaction / structure, so the setup is invalidated if the level truly fails.
  • TP1 / TP2 / TP3 — placed at prior structure (real support/resistance), falling back to measured R-multiples when there's clean air. The headline R:R is measured to TP2.
  • Scale & trail — partial profits are booked at each TP (e.g. 25% / 50% / 15%); the stop moves to break-even at TP1, then below new support at TP2, and never moves against the trade.

Paper-trade journal & track record

Armed A+/A setups are logged to a forward-test journal at the live (delayed) intraday price during market hours, then marked to market each scan — booking scale-outs and stops exactly as the rules dictate. Every trade carries its entry-type label, timeframe and grade, so the Track page can show real expectancy split by entry type, grade and timeframe.

PULSE & liquidity

The PULSE bar across the top tracks macro context — gold, silver, oil, gas, the tech and biotech indices, bond yields and the Aussie dollar — each with its 1-day and 5-day move. A liquidity filter keeps only names trading meaningful volume each day.

Timing & data

Scans refresh automatically through the session. Three markets are covered: ASX, NASDAQ and Crypto (which trades 24/7). Price and volume come from Yahoo Finance (dividend-adjusted, ~15 min delayed) — adequate for the daily and weekly bars this system trades on.
