📖 Explain Like I'm 5 — Understanding Markets & Sectors

What is a sector?

The stock market is divided into 11 sectors — groups of companies that do similar things. Banks live in "Financials", Apple lives in "Technology", Woolworths lives in "Consumer Staples". When a sector rises, money is flowing into those businesses. When it falls, money is flowing out.

What is sector rotation?

Money in the market is always moving. When investors feel confident, money flows into risky sectors (Tech, Discretionary). When they feel scared, it rotates into safe sectors (Utilities, Healthcare, Gold). Spotting where the money is flowing tells you what phase the market is in — and which setups are most likely to work.

How do I use this page?

Look for sectors showing green — those have momentum. Only trade stocks in sectors that are trending up. If you're in a pullback setup and the whole sector is falling, your trade fights the wind. Always trade with the sector trend, not against it.

What is "relative strength"?

A stock has relative strength (RS) when it rises more than the market when the market goes up, and falls less when it drops. The best setups combine strong RS and a strong sector behind them — double tailwind. These produce much higher probability trades.

Bull vs Bear market signals

Bull signal: Tech, Financials and Discretionary leading → risk-on, pullback setups shine.
Bear signal: Utilities, Healthcare, Gold leading → risk-off, reduce size, favour shorts or cash.
Mixed signals: No clear sector leader → reduce position size, trade only A+ setups.

The golden rule

Don't fight the tape. If the sector is going down, your long trade swims upstream. Wait for the sector to stabilise or rotate up before entering. Patience in weak sectors is not fear — it's discipline. The best trades feel almost boring because everything is aligned.
